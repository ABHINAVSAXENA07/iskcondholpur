Introduction to ISKCON

The Hare Krishna movement, formally known as the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, was founded by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada in July 1966 in New York. ISKCON is rooted in the Gaudiya-Vaishnava lineage, a monotheistic branch of the Vedic tradition. Over time, ISKCON has flourished, expanding to include more than 400 temples, 40 rural communities, and a network of over 100 vegetarian restaurants. The organization also carries out significant global initiatives, such as the renowned "Food for Life" program, which stands as the world's sole free vegetarian relief effort.

A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Srila Prabhupada (1896–1977) was a spiritual leader, scholar, and founder of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), also known as the Hare Krishna Movement. He is widely regarded as one of the foremost proponents of bhakti-yoga and the teachings of Lord Krishna in modern times.
